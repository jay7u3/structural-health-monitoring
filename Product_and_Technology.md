# II. Products & Technology

## Product Description

Our project is not a sensor product, a material product, or a standalone AI algorithm. It is an integrated platform we call an AI-assisted Infrastructure Health Intelligence Platform — a long-term structural health intelligence layer for aging buildings, bridges, and heritage infrastructure. We do not simply detect structural damage; we help infrastructure owners understand how their buildings age over time.

Structural Health Monitoring is already a mature field. Ultrasonic methods, vibration analysis, visual inspection and manual auditing all exist, and we are not pretending to reinvent any of them. The real problem is different: today's systems collect data, but they cannot interpret long-term structural change. Three pain points dominate the industry.

The first is monitoring blind spots. Point-based sensors only cover local areas, so sparse deployment leaves dangerous gaps while dense deployment explodes cost, data volume and maintenance complexity. The second is the false-alarm explosion. Even with a 95 % detection rate and a 5 % false-positive rate in the lab, real damage is rare — roughly a 1 % base rate — so by Bayes' theorem more than 80 % of field alarms are false positives. With a thousand sensors checked hourly at a 1 % rate, that means around 240 false alarms per day, which is operationally unusable. The third pain point is the absence of a long-term health profile. Most systems can only say "something looks abnormal here." They cannot tell whether the anomaly is transient noise or a real aging trend, which zones are degrading fastest, what the risk will look like in five years, or where a limited maintenance budget should go first.

The market does not lack data — it lacks structural interpretation. Our product is built around that gap and is organized in four layers. At the bottom, a distributed smart sensing layer attaches to the host structure as a modular skin and collects acoustic, vibration or ultrasonic signals at distributed points, eliminating the blind spots of spot sensors while staying non-invasive. Above it, an AI signal interpretation engine handles the noisy reality of real structures — wind, rain, temperature swings, vehicle vibration, foot traffic, thermal expansion — and learns each structure's normal behavioral pattern instead of asking the trivial "crack or no crack" question. The third layer is the building health profile: a continuous record of each structure's state that behaves like a medical record for a building, including a health score, a risk map, aging speed, degradation zones, anomaly and maintenance history, and environmental correlations. On top sits the decision platform, the customer-facing dashboard and reporting layer that turns the health profile into prioritized maintenance decisions. A building should not only be inspected — it should have a memory of its own structural health.

## Core Technology

The sensing layer relies on ultrasonic propagation. A transducer placed on the material surface emits a wave that travels through the structure; whenever the wave meets an interface between two media of different acoustic impedances, part of the energy reflects and is picked up by the same or a paired sensor, creating an echo. For two-sided parts, defect detection compares the time the wave needs to cross the thickness against the time it needs to reflect off a defect. We prefer ultrasound to passive acoustic emission because the echo time directly encodes geometry, whereas acoustic emission only "listens" once damage is already speaking.

We are evaluating three hardware variants. The Digital Smart Layer ensures near-zero signal loss over long distances, lets many sensors share a single cable, can self-diagnose faults and emits strong ultrasonic signals; its downside is that it relies on delicate microchips, cannot tolerate extreme heat, and must be glued onto relatively flat surfaces. Piezoelectric paint, applied as a spray-on coating, follows any complex shape, hidden corner or weld without adding weight and survives much higher temperatures, but it produces weaker ultrasonic signals, still requires wiring at each point, and needs a difficult high-voltage activation step on site. A hybrid approach combines the spray-on coverage with the digital layer's wiring backbone, keeping shape adaptability without losing signal quality.

Energy autonomy is an active research track. Piezoelectric and triboelectric harvesters can convert structural micro-vibrations into electricity, opening the way to a self-powered patch. Reported EO-TENG numbers reach an open-circuit voltage of about 105 V and a short-circuit current of about 58 µA under a 2.4 cm / 1 Hz excitation, with up to 66 nC of transferred charge. With an integrated power-management circuit, the array can drive an on-board data-acquisition and NB-IoT communication module without external power, removing power supply as a deployment constraint.

The AI interpretation engine sits on top of these signals. It is trained jointly on ultrasonic responses and environmental conditions — temperature, humidity, pressure, brightness, weather category, and dated context such as season — so that wind, rain, traffic or natural thermal expansion no longer trigger false alarms. Rather than producing a flood of raw alerts, it ranks anomalies by severity, learns a per-structure baseline because every building has its own "normal", and tracks how that baseline drifts over months and years to model aging trends. Crucially, it also tries to attribute anomalies to plausible causes: differential settlement on unstable ground, drought-and-rehydration cycles in the soil, water ingress, or thermal expansion between roughly −10 °C and +40 °C. The engine's outputs feed the per-asset health profile and, ultimately, the dashboard that turns continuous signal interpretation into prioritized maintenance actions.

## Product Innovation

The first innovation is a paradigm shift, from damage detection to aging intelligence. Traditional SHM stops at "something is wrong"; our platform turns continuous signals into a long-term aging profile of the structure, and that profile is what enables a move from reactive alarms to preventive decisions.

The second is prevention itself. A pure sensor confirms damage only after it has occurred. Our sensing skin continuously listens for tiny vibrations humans cannot detect and, combined with the interpretation engine, can flag risk before the damage becomes irreversible.

Closely related is the third innovation: cause explanation packaged into a building-health score. Beyond raw alarms, the platform attributes anomalies to candidate causes — micro-cracks versus structural cracks versus fissures, soil settlement, water ingress, thermal expansion, environmental noise — and rolls those into a single comparable score on a defined scale. The point is not to impress engineers but to give a non-engineer manager a number they can act on, which is the basis of our pitch line: "How is the health of your house?"

The fourth innovation lives in the hardware: a distributed sensing skin instead of point sensors. By moving from discrete probes to a continuous (or near-continuous) layer, we remove the blind spots between traditional spot sensors without exploding cost or maintenance complexity. The hybrid spray-on plus digital-layer concept lets us adapt to complex shapes, hidden corners and welds that point sensors cannot cover. The fifth innovation, self-powered operation through vibration harvesting, makes that distributed deployment realistic over years rather than weeks.

The sixth innovation is less visible but the most strategic: a long-term data moat. Every new deployment feeds a closed learning loop — monitoring produces data, data trains the AI, better predictions enable better maintenance, and the resulting outcomes generate more data. Over time we accumulate aging patterns per material, long-term signal evolution per structure type, environmental-to-anomaly correlations, before-and-after-maintenance signal changes, and feedback between risk warnings and the actual repairs they triggered. This is what upgrades the AI from "detecting anomalies" to "understanding aging mechanisms", and it is something competitors cannot reproduce by reading our marketing material.

Finally, the business architecture itself is an innovation. Every building is different, but the system logic is the same. We standardize the sensing module logic, data pipeline, AI analysis framework, dashboard interface, health-score methodology and reporting format; we adapt sensor placement, coverage density, material choice, monitoring frequency, risk thresholds and reporting needs to each site. The result is a standardized intelligence platform with adaptive deployment, much closer to how electronic health records handle different patients with one underlying system than to a custom-engineering shop.

## Application Objects

The platform is designed for any critical structure whose silent aging is hard to inspect manually but expensive to lose. Heritage monuments are a natural first target — the Leaning Tower of Pisa is the obvious illustration: instead of carrying out blind, periodic restoration, owners can take targeted measures based on actual evolution data. Bridges, especially aging or heavily loaded ones, fit the same logic, because internal evolution between visual inspections is exactly what current methods miss. High-rise buildings benefit from continuous tilt and drift monitoring over years rather than one-off audits. Critical at-risk roads and unstable ground areas, such as the Bangkok subsidence case, fall into the same family because ground instability propagates into surface infrastructure. The platform is also relevant for buildings used as shelters in conflict zones, where early detection of tilt or imminent collapse risk has obvious human stakes.

On the customer side, three segments map cleanly onto the product. Municipalities and other public-asset owners face limited maintenance budgets and direct public-safety accountability; what they buy from us is the ability to prioritize interventions, reduce uncertainty and manage public risk — in short, to spend maintenance budgets where they matter most. Heritage institutions face irreversible damage to non-replaceable cultural assets; what they buy is non-invasive long-term monitoring, early aging detection and a structural health archive, so they can preserve before irreversible damage happens. Maintenance companies face labor-intensive inspection work and a hard-to-differentiate offer; what they buy is a data-driven service upgrade, visual reports and longer client relationships, turning one-off inspection into a recurring intelligence service.

## Product Advantages

We do not compete head-on with sensor manufacturers. We build a structural-health intelligence layer on top of their data, and the resulting advantages over traditional SHM are concrete enough to be summarized in one table.

| Dimension | Traditional SHM | Our Platform |
|---|---|---|
| Data collection | Point-based | Distributed |
| Time logic | Periodic | Continuous |
| Output | Alarms | Health profile |
| Decision value | Requires expert interpretation | Supports maintenance planning |
| Main focus | Damage detection | Aging intelligence |
| Business value | Inspection support | Long-term risk reduction |

In practice this means a deployment without blind spots, far fewer false alarms because the AI filters environmental noise and ranks remaining anomalies, and a continuous picture of how a structure changes rather than a snapshot of how it is today. The sensing layer is non-invasive enough to suit heritage assets where drilling and bolting are unacceptable, and it is designed for a multi-year unattended lifespan thanks to energy harvesting and modular hardware. The output is decision-ready — a health score, a risk map, prioritized maintenance recommendations — usable directly by non-engineer managers, not raw waveforms that demand an expert in the loop. And because the platform tries to explain the cause of an anomaly rather than only flag it, owners can act on the right problem instead of chasing every alarm.

The positioning that summarizes all of the above is that we are an AI-assisted infrastructure health intelligence platform that helps municipalities and heritage institutions transform structural monitoring data into long-term aging profiles and preventive maintenance decisions.
